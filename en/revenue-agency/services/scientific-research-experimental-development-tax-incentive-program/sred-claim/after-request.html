Review process

A Research and Technology Advisor (RTA) will review your information, meet with you to discuss your application, and provide you with a decision.

  1. Get assigned an RTA

    After you have submitted your application for pre-claim approval, an RTA will contact the person indicated in box 034 of the application form.

    The RTA will phone to:

    • Confirm your official language of choice
    • Explain the pre-claim approval process
    • Explain the SR&ED programâ€¯requirements and how a project can receive pre-claim approval
    • Answer your questions about SR&ED pre-claim approval requirements
    • Discuss any aspects of your proposed project that may require more information or clarification
    • Set-up a meeting that will take virtually or on site, when your staff with in-depth knowledge of the proposed SR&ED work are available

    After the phone call, you will receive an email in your CRA account that will summarize what was discussed during the call and will provide both:

    • An agenda for the upcoming meeting with an estimated time commitment (between 2 to 5 hours)
    • The contact information for the RTA and their manager

  2. Prepare to meet with your RTA

    We recommend you:

    • Ensure the availability of the individuals you choose to participate in the meeting, including the technical expert from the business
    • Be prepared to discuss:
      • The existing state of knowledge or technology as pertains to your proposed project
      • The scientific or technological uncertainty the project is aiming to address
      • The work to be carried out during the requested pre-claim approval period to overcome the scientific or the technological uncertainty
      • How you plan to structure your systematic investigation or search, including initial hypothesis and planned experiments or analyses
    • Confirm you have received the invitation for the virtual meeting (if applicable)

  3. Meet with your RTA

    The meeting will be a discussion between the RTA and the business, to provide the RTA with any details required to determine whether the work in the planned project meets the definition of SR&ED.

    To better understand your business, the RTA may request a tour of your place of business or manufacturing premises during the meeting. This can be done either virtually or on-site. A tour provides an opportunity for the RTA to visualize and contextualize your business environment.

    If the tour is being conducted virtually, you can present your work in a virtual manner by:

    • Presenting slides or other documents through screen sharing during a videoconference
    • Using a camera-equipped laptop or mobile device to take the meeting participants on a tour of the facility

    At the end of the meeting, your RTA will provide a verbal summary of:

    • Their understanding of the scientific or technological uncertainty and your planned approach to address it
    • The additional information or clarifications they obtained during the meeting
    • Any areas where they still have concerns and require further clarification (if applicable)
    • Recommendations for future applications or SR&ED claims

Find out if you are approved

Your RTA will communicate their decision to you by phone or videoconference before sending it to you in writing.

The pre-claim approval report will:

  • Provide confirmation on whether the project is granted pre-approval
  • Include details and a rationale on the decision, and may provide you with recommendations to help you with future applications or SR&ED claims

If changes are made to the proposed project

If your project is granted pre-claim approval, and over the course of the pre-claim approval period you make material changes to your project, we encourage you to reach out to the RTA or contact us.

Disagreeing with CRA's decision

There is no appeal process for pre-claim approval.

If your pre-claim approval is denied and you do not agree with the decision, you can talk with the RTA or their manager to try to resolve any disputes.

If you are not able to resolve the dispute(s), you may make material changes to the proposed project and either:

  • Re-submit an application for pre-claim approval with the changes to the proposed project
  • Move forward without pre-claim approval and submit an SR&ED claim for the project after undertaking the work

Get support after you apply

Contact us if you have any questions about the SR&ED program or your application for pre-claim approval.
